Rhyme

Time limit 1 second
Memory limit 64.0 MB
Input stdin or input.txt
Output stdout or output.txt

The K K K-th queen of Berland is ascending to the throne. Traditionally, a poem is read out at the coronation in honor of the new monarch. Of course, the lines in the poem must rhyme. Each new ruler wants their poem to be more beautiful than the ones that came before, which means the lines in it have to rhyme even better. The K K K-th queen of Berland considers two lines to rhyme if the following two conditions are met:

  • Both lines are at least K K K characters long.
  • The last K K K characters in both lines match.

Eventually K K K got so large that checking if two lines rhyme by hand started taking up too much time. Now it is your job as court programmer.

Input format

An integer K K K ( 1 ≤ K ≤ 1 0 5 1 \leq K \leq 10^5 1≤K≤105) is given in the first line, followed by two strings 0 0 0 to 1 0 5 10^5 105 characters long (inclusive), each on a separate line. The strings may contain uppercase and lowercase Latin letters, digits, spaces and ASCII punctuation (!"#$%&'()*+,-./:;<=>?@[\]^_`{|}~).

Output format

Output YES if the two lines rhyme or NO otherwise.

Sample 1

Input Output 
2
palka
seledka

YES

Sample 2

Input Output 
3
palka
seledka

NO

Sample 3

Input Output 
1000
palka
palka

NO
